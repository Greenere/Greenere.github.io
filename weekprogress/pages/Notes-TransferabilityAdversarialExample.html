Conclusions

Generating Adversarial Exmaples

Most of the methods can be classified into three basic categories.(Categorized by paper 1)

  1. Optimization-based Approach

    Directly solve the problem by optimizing the loss.

    Minimize the differences between original input and adversarial example while maxmizing the loss of the correct classification of .

  2. Fast Gradient Sign

    "Push" the original input against the correct direction for classification, resulting the adversarial example .

  3. Fast Gradient

    Similarly to Fast Gradient Sign, but push by the gradient instead of gradient sign.

These methods have iterative versions, which iteratively applies the processes to the original input. The iterative version generally outperform the vanilla version in the white-box scenario on the expense of transferability.

Improving the Transferability of the Adversarial Examples

Ensemble Attack

Ensemble attack has been proven to be useful to improve the transferability of adversarial examples, which attacks a group of different classifiers simultaneously. This is applied by all of these papers above to improve the performance of their basic methods.

Input Diversity

In paper 2, the authors apply random and differentiable transformations to the input images with probability to increase the input diversity and maximize the loss function over these transformed inputs, thus alleviating the overfitting phenomenon. More specifically, they use random resizing and random padding as the transformations.

Momentum

In paper 4, the authors introduce the momentum from the deep learning training to the generating of adversarial examples, thus reducing the probability of falling into local minima.

This method is named as MI-FSGM

Translation-Invariant Attack

In paper 3, the authors found that the defense models make predictions based on different discriminative regions compared with normally trained models. To mitigate the effect of it, they generate an adversarial example of an ensemble of images composed of a legitimate one and its translated versions.

The direct calculation is computationally expensive, however, they introduce a kernel matrix on the presumption that the CNNs is translation-invariant.

Inspirations

The ensemble attack is very natural and direct, which imrpoves the transferability on the cost of computational resources.

Basically, the generating of transferable adversarial examples is treated as an optimization problem by these papers, thus the methods used in other deep-learning-related problems like momentum and diverse inputs can be applied.

The translation-invariant attack takes advantage of the feature of defense models, thus deducing the idea of translation-invariation. The later reformation of the problem makes it a feasible path to do some further improvements.
